# Review Questions — Section 213: Mobilization/Emergency Incidents

**Instructions:** Answer each question, then expand the details to check your answer.

---

## Question 1
Who has the authority to activate a Mass Arrest Processing Center (MAPC)?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

The **Chief of Department** activates the MAPC. At First Amendment events specifically, only the **First Amendment Activity Senior Executive or designee** can authorize MAPC activation. (P.G. 213-06)

</details>

---

## Question 2
What is the maximum number of prisoners that can be transported per wagon during mass arrest processing?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**20 prisoners** per wagon. (P.G. 213-06)

</details>

---

## Question 3
What is the maximum arrest processing time for violations and misdemeanors at a First Amendment activity?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**7 hours 30 minutes.** (P.G. 213-06)

</details>

---

## Question 4
An arresting officer during mass arrest processing can be assigned no more than how many prisoners? And an arrest supervisor can be assigned no more than how many arresting officers?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

An arresting officer processes no more than **5 prisoners**. An arrest supervisor is assigned no more than **4 arresting officers**. (P.G. 213-06)

</details>

---

## Question 5
At the MAPC, prisoners must be provided food after being held for how long?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Food must be provided if a prisoner is held **more than 2 hours**. (P.G. 213-06)

</details>

---

## Question 6
Who is the Incident Commander at a special event that spans multiple patrol boroughs?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

The **Chief of Patrol** is the Incident Commander for multi-borough events. For multi-command (single borough) events, it is the **Patrol Borough Commander**. For single command events, it is the **Precinct Commanding Officer**. (P.G. 213-15)

</details>

---

## Question 7
What are the barrier exclusion distances at a parade route?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**100 feet** exclusion from the parade route on through streets, and **25 feet** on the parade route from corners. Additionally, barrier configuration must NOT unreasonably restrict pedestrian access. (P.G. 213-15)

</details>

---

## Question 8
What is the current citywide security alert level for New York City, and how are changes communicated?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

NYC is maintained at **ORANGE (High)**, which is the fourth of five levels. Changes are communicated via **FINEST Message**. (P.G. 213-18)

</details>

---

## Question 9
List the five citywide security alert levels in order from lowest to highest, including their color and name.

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

1. **GREEN** — Low
2. **BLUE** — Guarded
3. **YELLOW** — Elevated
4. **ORANGE** — High
5. **RED** — Severe

(P.G. 213-18)

</details>

---

## Question 10
How are NLG legal observers and NYCLU legal observers identified at demonstrations?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**NLG (National Lawyers Guild)** legal observers wear **bright green hats**. **NYCLU** legal observers wear **blue hats/vests**. Both must be permitted **free access through police lines**. (P.G. 213-20)

</details>

---

## Question 11
Who has the authority to order mass arrests at a First Amendment activity? Can a sergeant order mass arrests?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Only a **Senior Executive (deputy chief or higher)** or a **designee (captain or above)** can order mass arrests at First Amendment activities. A sergeant **cannot** order mass arrests. However, individual arrests for criminal conduct are still permitted by any supervisor. (P.G. 213-20)

</details>

---

## Question 12
What are the three required handcuff check points during First Amendment arrest processing?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

1. **Entering** the transport van
2. **Exiting** the transport van
3. **Regularly** at arrest processing

(P.G. 213-20)

</details>

---

## Question 13
Under what circumstances can body-worn cameras be activated at demonstrations?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Body-worn cameras are **NOT activated** at demonstrations **unless** per P.G. 212-123. The mere occurrence of a protest does not authorize camera activation. (P.G. 213-20)

</details>

---

## Question 14
What is the Handschu Consent Decree, and what does it require?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

The Handschu Consent Decree requires **written approval from the Intelligence and Counterterrorism Bureau** for political activity investigations. It is a legal constraint limiting investigative authority at First Amendment events. (P.G. 213-20)

</details>

---

## Question 15
What role can a sergeant play in a Tier One response to a First Amendment activity?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

At a **Tier One** response, a sergeant may serve as the **Incident Commander**. Higher-tier responses require higher-ranking Incident Commanders. (P.G. 213-20)

</details>

---

## Question 16
Regarding protest liaisons from the Community Affairs Bureau (CAB), what are the uniform and assignment restrictions?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

CAB protest liaisons **must** wear uniform clearly displaying rank, name, shield, and command. They may **NOT** wear civilian attire. CAB personnel are **NOT** assigned to patrol or enforcement duties except in **exceptional circumstances**. (P.G. 213-20)

</details>

---

## Question 17
How often must the Unusual Disorder Kit be inspected, and in which months?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

The Unusual Disorder Kit requires **semiannual inspection** in **January** and **July**. (P.G. 213-09)

</details>

---

## Question 18
How many sector maps, index cards, and tags must be in an Unusual Disorder Kit?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

- **75** sector maps
- **200** index cards
- **300** tags

The kit also contains 200 Complaint Report Worksheets, 200 Aided Report Worksheets, and 200 Detail Roster sheets. (P.G. 213-09)

</details>

---

## Question 19
Which unit must be notified for all events affecting traffic, and which additional unit must be notified for unscheduled events?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

The **Traffic Management Center (TMC)** must be notified of **all** events affecting traffic. The **Operations Unit** must also be notified for **unscheduled** events. (P.G. 213-17)

</details>

---

## Question 20
What steps must be taken before enforcement action at a First Amendment activity?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

Before enforcement action at a First Amendment activity:
1. **De-escalation** attempts are required
2. **Warnings** must be given (when feasible)
3. **Sufficient resources** must be assembled before taking enforcement action
4. Only a **Senior Executive or designee** can order mass arrests

Individual arrests for criminal conduct may still be made by any supervisor without these prerequisites. (P.G. 213-20)

</details>

---

## Question 21
During a special event, the Incident Commander determines that additional resources are needed. For a multi-borough event, who serves as the Incident Commander?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

For a special event that spans **multiple patrol boroughs**, the **Chief of Patrol** serves as the Incident Commander. For multi-command events within a single borough, it is the **Patrol Borough Commander**. For single-command events, it is the **Precinct Commanding Officer**. (P.G. 213-15)

</details>

---

## Question 22
What is the minimum number of days before a special event that the Traffic Management Center must be notified?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

The Traffic Management Center must be notified at least **7 days** prior to the event. For unscheduled events, notification must be made as soon as practicable, and the Operations Unit must also be notified. (P.G. 213-17)

</details>

---

## Question 23
At a First Amendment activity, a sergeant observes protesters blocking traffic. Can the sergeant order mass arrests to clear the roadway?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**No.** Only a **Senior Executive (deputy chief or higher)** or a **designee (captain or above)** can order mass arrests at First Amendment activities. A sergeant cannot order mass arrests. However, the sergeant may order **individual arrests** for criminal conduct (such as disorderly conduct) without higher approval. (P.G. 213-20)

</details>

---

## Question 24
How often must the Unusual Disorder Kit be inspected, and who is responsible for the inspection?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

The Unusual Disorder Kit requires **semiannual inspection** in **January** and **July**. The **patrol supervisor** is responsible for ensuring the kit is inspected and all contents are accounted for. (P.G. 213-09)

</details>

---

## Question 25
What is the maximum number of prisoners that can be assigned to a single arresting officer during mass arrest processing?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

An arresting officer can be assigned no more than **5 prisoners** during mass arrest processing. An arrest supervisor can be assigned no more than **4 arresting officers** to supervise. (P.G. 213-06)

</details>

---

## Question 26
A prisoner has been in processing at the MAPC for 90 minutes without food. Is this a violation of procedure?

<details><summary>Answer</summary>

**Yes.** Prisoners must be provided food if held **more than 2 hours** at the MAPC. At 90 minutes, food is not yet required, but the sergeant should monitor the time to ensure food is provided before the 2-hour mark is reached. (P.G. 213-06)

</details>
