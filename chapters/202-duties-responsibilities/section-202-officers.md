# Section 202 — Officer-Level Duties & Responsibilities

> **Source:** NYPD Patrol Guide, Section 202 (P.G. 202-01 through 202-17)
> **Exam Relevance:** Sergeants must know these duties to supervise, correct, and evaluate subordinates.

---

## P.G. 202-01: Police Officer

**Key Duties:**
- Perform duty in uniform as indicated on roll call
- Monitor portable radio at all times while on patrol
- Proceed to assigned post/sector as directed by supervisor
- Do NOT congregate or engage in unnecessary conversation while on post
- Report unusual crime/occurrence to both the patrol supervisor AND the platoon commander

**Additional Requirements:**
- Report conditions not requiring immediate attention to the switchboard operator
- Maintain a digital Activity Log throughout the tour
- Do NOT leave post/sector until meal actually commences (travel time is NOT authorized)

**Sergeant Relevance:**
A patrol supervisor must verify that officers are on post, properly uniformed, monitoring radios, and maintaining their Activity Logs. The prohibition against congregating is a supervisory enforcement point — the patrol supervisor is specifically tasked with ensuring members do not congregate or engage in unnecessary conversation (see P.G. 202-19). The meal rule (no early departure for travel time) is a common discipline issue.

---

## P.G. 202-02: Radio Motor Patrol (RMP) Operator

**Key Duties:**
- Inspect seatbelts before tour; notify patrol supervisor of any deficiencies
- Wear safety belts at all times (may be removed ONLY in dangerous tactical situations)
- Inspect Ballistic Window Inserts at start of tour
- Permit ONLY uniformed MOS performing related police duty to enter the RMP
- Respond to any serious emergency within 5 blocks, even if directed to another assignment

**Additional Requirements:**
- Approach the scene of a reported crime quietly — do NOT use siren unnecessarily
- Do NOT carry electric blasting caps in vehicles
- Do NOT tow or push vehicles with the RMP (exception: Highway District units equipped with protective bumper guards)

**Sergeant Relevance:**
The patrol supervisor must evaluate driving habits and take corrective action. Seatbelt deficiencies must be reported to the platoon commander; seatbelt vandalism must be reported to the Internal Affairs Bureau. The 5-block rule for serious emergencies is a frequently tested concept. Supervisors should use AVL/DAS to monitor RMP locations.

---

## P.G. 202-03: Radio Motor Patrol (RMP) Recorder

**Key Duties:**
- Ride in the front passenger seat (exception: move to rear when transporting a prisoner or EDP in a vehicle without a partition)
- Notify radio dispatcher when any non-MOS enters the RMP (report time, gender, and beginning mileage)
- Search the passenger area for contraband after transporting any non-MOS
- Carry a duplicate set of keys for the vehicle at all times

**Sergeant Relevance:**
Supervisors should verify that recorders are properly positioned in the front seat and that required notifications are made to the dispatcher when civilians are transported. The post-transport search requirement is a liability and evidence-integrity issue that supervisors must enforce.

---

## P.G. 202-04: Motor Scooter Operator

**Key Duties:**
- Operate motor scooter in accordance with Department guidelines and traffic laws
- Wear required protective equipment (helmet, reflective vest) as prescribed
- Perform patrol functions similar to foot patrol but with extended range
- Report mechanical deficiencies to the patrol supervisor promptly

**Sergeant Relevance:**
Supervisors must ensure operators wear all required safety equipment and that scooters are properly maintained. Equipment deficiency reports flow through the patrol supervisor to the platoon commander.

---

## P.G. 202-05: Neighborhood Safety Team (NST) Officer

**Key Duties:**
- Wear specifically prescribed uniform; must identify as police BEFORE taking enforcement action
- Deploy based on crime trends and statistics (data-driven deployment)
- No more than 3 NST members (including the supervisor) per vehicle
- Must attend NST training before deployment
- Do NOT perform duty in plainclothes or conduct decoy operations

**Sergeant Relevance:**
This is a high-priority area for sergeants. NST teams may NOT deploy without a sergeant or above solely assigned to supervision (see P.G. 202-20). The 3-per-vehicle cap and the prohibition on plainclothes/decoy operations are strict rules. Failure to complete required training before deployment is a supervisory accountability issue.

## P.G. 202-06: Administrative Quality of Life Officer (05/05/26)

**Key Duties:**
- Report to Quality of Life Team Sergeant
- Perform duty in uniform
- Assist QOL Team Sergeant by preparing statistical reports under guidance of Chief of Department's Office
- Maintain master file of contact information for key community members/groups
- Advise team members on traffic related issues and concerns
- Review and analyze all Police Crash Reports (MV-104P); note deficiencies and bring to training sergeant's attention
- Duplicate and forward collision reports daily to Highway Unit for collisions on limited access highways
- Duplicate and forward collision reports to other agencies (DMV, TLC, etc.) when necessary
- Fax copy of all "crossover" collision reports to Corporation Counsel and Highway District
  - **Crossover collision:** Motor vehicle collision on surface road/highway where vehicle strikes/crosses median (jersey barrier, guard rail, divider, fence, etc.) separating opposing traffic
- Analyze and process TRAFFIC INTELLIGENCE REPORTS (PD178-150)
- Forward TRAFFIC INTELLIGENCE REPORTS that cannot be addressed at precinct level to Patrol Borough Traffic Safety Coordinator
- Identify collision-prone locations and determine contributing factors
- Survey all collision-prone locations and determine contributing factors
- Recommend necessary engineering, enforcement, and education strategies for collision reduction
- Forward enforcement information (days/hours collisions occurring, contributing factors) to other units (SRG, Highway Units, Citywide Traffic Task Force)
- Maintain liaison with DOT regarding engineering recommendations
- Issue appropriate summonses at collision-prone locations
- Count, record by category (red lights, improper turn, etc.), and process relevant summonses
- Enter Moving summons info into FORMS using "Paper" option daily
- Maintain and update precinct Traffic Safety Plan and Emergency Plans "A," "B," and "C"
- Evaluate and monitor precinct Pedestrian Safety Program
- Attend patrol borough traffic safety meetings
- Compile statistical data for Traffic Stat meetings; attend with executive officer when precinct scheduled
- Attend community council and Safety Board meetings to discuss traffic safety (as directed by CO)
- Coordinate activities with Community Affairs Officer to reduce collisions involving children
- Coordinate traffic-related training with training sergeant
- Prepare and/or forward all traffic-related reports
- Review HIGHWAY CONDITION RECORD (PD311-151) for conditions requiring agency notification
- Prepare MISSING TRAFFIC CONTROL DEVICE REPORT (PD620-150) and forward to appropriate DOT Borough Commissioner
- Maintain and update precinct traffic safety bulletin board
- Serve on Precinct Vehicle Pursuit and Collision Safety Review Board
- Review summonses for moving violations and violations returnable to Criminal Court/OATH
  - Identify summonses issued to TLC-licensed vehicles/owners
  - Reproduce 2 photocopies, enter sequential number from "Report of Violation" log
  - Forward 1 photocopy to TLC; file remaining copy in "Report of Violation" log

**Sergeant Relevance:**
This is a critical administrative role supporting the command's traffic safety program. The Administrative QOL Officer must NOT be used as backfill for other assignments without CO authorization. Key exam points: crossover collision definition, FORMS entry requirements, Traffic Safety Plan maintenance, and the prohibition on using this officer for unrelated duties. Supervisors should verify summonses are processed correctly and TLC notifications are made.

## P.G. 202-08: Youth Coordination Officer

**Key Duties:**
- Serve as the command's liaison for juvenile and youth-related matters
- Coordinate with schools, youth organizations, and community groups
- Maintain awareness of youth crime trends within the command
- Assist in juvenile-related investigations and referrals as needed

**Sergeant Relevance:**
Supervisors should ensure the Youth Coordination Officer is properly interfacing with community stakeholders and that juvenile matters are handled in compliance with P.G. 215 (Juvenile Matters). The desk officer should be aware of youth-related incidents reported through this officer.

---

## P.G. 202-09: Arrest Processing Officer

**Key Duties:**
- Process arrests within the command in accordance with Department procedures
- Maintain arrest processing area in orderly condition
- Ensure all required paperwork, notifications, and system entries are completed
- Coordinate with the desk officer regarding prisoner status and processing timelines

**Sergeant Relevance:**
The desk officer directly supervises the arrest processing officer (P.G. 202-18). Supervisors must inspect the arrest processing area and prisoner detention areas. Timeliness and accuracy of arrest processing is a supervisory responsibility.

---

## P.G. 202-10: Community Affairs Officer

**Key Duties:**
- Attend community council meetings as the command representative
- Establish and maintain contact with civic and community groups
- Maintain liaison with the special operations lieutenant
- Administer the MedicAlert and Alzheimer's Safe Return programs

**Sergeant Relevance:**
Supervisors should ensure the Community Affairs Officer is attending required meetings and maintaining productive community relationships. The liaison with the special operations lieutenant is an important chain-of-communication link. The MedicAlert/Alzheimer's programs have specific administrative requirements.

---

## P.G. 202-12: Command Auxiliary Police Coordinator

**Key Duties:**
- Coordinate the scheduling, deployment, and supervision of Auxiliary Police within the command
- Ensure Auxiliary Police members are properly trained and equipped
- Maintain records of Auxiliary Police participation and activity
- Report any incidents involving Auxiliary members to the desk officer

**Sergeant Relevance:**
Supervisors must understand the limitations of Auxiliary Police authority and ensure they are not deployed in situations beyond their scope. Any incidents involving Auxiliary members require notification up the chain through the desk officer.

---

## P.G. 202-13: Evidence/Property Control Specialist

**Key Duties:**
- Maintain the command's property/evidence storage area in secure, organized condition
- Process invoiced property in accordance with P.G. 218 (Property — General)
- Ensure chain of custody documentation is accurate and complete
- Coordinate property pickups and transfers with appropriate units

**Sergeant Relevance:**
The desk officer must inspect the property locker at the commencement of each tour (P.G. 202-18). Supervisors are accountable for the integrity of the evidence/property system. Missing or improperly documented property is a serious supervisory failure.

---

## P.G. 202-14: Telephone Switchboard Operator

**Key Duties:**
- Operate the command telephone switchboard, routing calls appropriately
- Record messages and relay information to the desk officer and patrol supervisor
- Receive reports of conditions not requiring immediate attention from officers on post
- Maintain a log of significant calls and notifications

**Sergeant Relevance:**
The desk officer directly supervises the switchboard operator (P.G. 202-18). The switchboard operator is a key information hub — supervisors should ensure calls are being routed properly and that important messages reach the appropriate personnel without delay.

---

## P.G. 202-15: Police Attendant — Uniformed/Civilian Member of the Service

**Key Duties:**
- Perform designated attendant duties within the command facility
- Assist the desk officer with administrative functions as directed
- Monitor designated areas of the command facility
- Report any irregularities or security concerns to the desk officer

**Sergeant Relevance:**
The desk officer directly supervises the police attendant (P.G. 202-18). Supervisors should ensure attendants are at their assigned positions and performing required duties throughout the tour.

---

## P.G. 202-16: Field Intelligence Officer (FIO)

**Key Duties:**
- Gather, analyze, and disseminate field intelligence relevant to the command
- Maintain intelligence files and databases in accordance with Department guidelines
- Coordinate with the Field Intelligence Sergeant on intelligence priorities
- Prepare intelligence reports and briefings for command staff

**Sergeant Relevance:**
The Field Intelligence Sergeant (P.G. 202-25) directly supervises this officer. Supervisors should ensure intelligence-gathering activities comply with Department guidelines and legal requirements. FIO information feeds into crime-trend analysis used for NST and patrol deployment decisions.

---

## P.G. 202-17: Patrolwagon Operator

**Key Duties:**
- Operate the patrolwagon for prisoner transport and other authorized purposes
- Inspect the vehicle at the start of each tour for deficiencies and cleanliness
- Ensure the security of prisoners during transport
- Coordinate transport requests through the desk officer

**Sergeant Relevance:**
The desk officer directly supervises the patrolwagon operator (P.G. 202-18). Prisoner transport security is a high-liability area. Supervisors must ensure the vehicle is properly inspected and that prisoner handling procedures are followed during every transport.
