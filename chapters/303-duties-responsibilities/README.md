## Source
- **Administrative Guide Section:** 303
- **Source PDF:** Public Admin Guide1.pdf
- **Priority:** High

## Overview

Section 303 of the Administrative Guide defines the duties and responsibilities of borough-level command staff. These five procedures establish the chain of command from Borough Commander down through the executive officers, adjutant, and operations commander. For the sergeant exam, the key takeaways are the reporting relationships, the distinction between operational and administrative oversight, and the specific timeframes for inspections/briefings.

## Priority Topics for Sergeant Exam

1. **Borough Commander (303-01)** — Semi-annual briefings, annual CPP inspections, quarterly meetings
2. **Operations vs. Administration split** — Operations exec handles crime/QOL/traffic; Administration exec handles discipline/personnel/scheduling/QA
3. **Borough Adjutant (303-04)** — Early Intervention Committee, Chronic Absent monitoring, CCRB conferral reviews, self-inspection program
4. **Reporting relationships** — Who reports to whom in the borough hierarchy
5. **Borough Operations Commander (303-05)** — 9 crime categories, youth coordination officer selection

## Study Tips

- Know the **two executive officer tracks**: Operations (303-02) handles tactical/enforcement matters; Administration (303-03) handles personnel/discipline/scheduling
- The Borough Adjutant (303-04) has the most sergeant-relevant duties — Early Intervention, Chronic Absent, CCRB reviews
- Memorize the Borough Commander's timeframes: **semi-annual** (IAB/CAB briefings), **annual** (CPP inspections), **quarterly** (executive meetings)
- The Operations Commander oversees **9 specific crime categories** — the exam may test whether you can identify which ones
